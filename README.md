# Greenhouse gas emissions 

## 📊 Project Summary

This project analyzes global and country-level CO₂ emissions using data visualization techniques, with a strong focus on Rwanda.

The analysis covers the period 2000–2020 and explores trends in carbon emissions, growth rates, population impact, and comparisons with other countries.

Key areas explored:
- Evolution of CO₂ emissions in Rwanda over time
- Year-to-year emission growth and volatility
- Relationship between population growth and emissions
- Cross-country comparisons based on similar population sizes
- CO₂ emissions per capita analysis
- Breakdown of greenhouse gases (CO₂, methane, nitrous oxide, fluorinated gases)
- Top emitting countries globally (2010–2020)
- Industry-level emissions (case study: China)
- Emissions by income group
- CO₂ embedded in international trade (import/export analysis)

## 💡 Key Insights

- Rwanda’s emissions remain relatively low despite population growth, with no clear direct correlation between the two
- Emissions peaked around 2019 (~1.19 million tonnes)
- High-income countries contribute disproportionately to global emissions  
- Coal is the dominant source of emissions in major economies like China (~73%)
- Global trade plays a major role in redistributing carbon emissions across countries  

This project demonstrates the use of data visualization to uncover environmental and economic patterns in global CO₂ emissions.
